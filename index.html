CRISTIANO RONALDO

Cristiano Ronaldo, born on 5 February 1985 in Funchal, Portugal, is a professional footballer who plays for Al-Nassr in the Saudi Pro League and captains the Portugal national team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest players in football history and holds the record for most goals in men's international football with over 130 goals.

Ronaldo began his career at Sporting CP before joining Manchester United in 2003. At United, he won three Premier League titles, the UEFA Champions League, and his first Ballon d'Or in 2008. His explosive pace, skill, and scoring ability quickly made him a global star.

In 2009, he moved to Real Madrid for a then-record fee and spent nine highly successful seasons there. He became the club’s all-time top scorer with 451 goals and won four Champions League titles, four Ballon d'Or awards, and numerous other trophies.

Ronaldo joined Juventus in 2018, winning two Serie A titles, before returning to Manchester United in 2021. In 2023, he transferred to Al-Nassr, becoming one of the first global stars to play in Saudi Arabia’s top league.

Internationally, Ronaldo has represented Portugal in multiple European Championships and World Cups. He led Portugal to victory at UEFA Euro 2016 and won the UEFA Nations League in 2019 and 2025. He holds the record for most appearances and goals in European Championship history.

With over 900 career goals for club and country, five Ballon d'Or awards, and numerous global records, Ronaldo is celebrated for his athleticism, longevity, and goal-scoring prowess. He remains a major influence in football and a global sports icon.

Major Achievements

  • 5 Ballon d'Or awards (2008, 2013, 2014, 2016, 2017)
  • UEFA Champions League titles: 5 (2008 with Manchester United; 2014, 2016, 2017, 2018 with Real Madrid)
  • European Championship winner: 2016 with Portugal
  • UEFA Nations League winner: 2019 and 2025 with Portugal
  • All-time top scorer for Real Madrid (451 goals)
